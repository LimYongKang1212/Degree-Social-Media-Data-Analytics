<h1 align="left">Final Project  - Hate Speech Among Social Media in Malaysia</h1>

###

<h2 align="left">Introduction</h2>

###

<p align="justify">Hate speech on social media has emerged as a concerning issue globally, including in Malaysia. Social media platforms, such as Facebook and Instagram, offer vast spaces for users to express their opinions and engage in discussions.</p>

###

<h2 align="left">Problem Statement</h2>

###

<p align="justify">In Malaysia, like in many other countries, the rise of hate speech on social media has raised alarm among policymakers, civil society organizations, and the general public. The anonymity and reach of social media platforms can amplify the spread of hate speech, making it crucial to address this issue promptly and effectively.</p>

###

<h2 align="left">Objective</h2>

###

<p align="justify">1. To identify the most common sentiment found in Malaysian social media hate speech<br><br>2. To identify the source, target, and relationship in social media hate speech in Malaysia</p>

###

<h2 align="left">Methodology</h2>

###

<p align="justify">This study includes five main steps: selecting relevant keywords, gathering data from Facebook and Instagram via Apify, preprocessing the textual data, analyzing sentiment and network connections, and displaying the findings. Data preparation includes cleaning text by removing stopwords, punctuation, emojis, and other irrelevant content. Sentiment analysis is performed in Python, while social network analysis is conducted in Gephi. The final findings are displayed via a Power BI dashboard.</p>

<h2 align="left">Results</h2>

###

<p align="justify">The results indicate that most social media posts exhibit negative sentiment, especially those containing hate-speech keywords such as “bodoh,” “rasuah,” and “cerai.” Negative posts gain the greatest response in terms of likes, comments, and shares. Network analysis reveals several local groups and shows that specific phrases are essential for spreading hate speech. The dashboard provides an interactive way to explore these patterns and insights.</p>

<h2 align="left">Conclusion</h2>

###

<p align="justify">hate speech is widely present on social media in Malaysia and is mostly negative in nature. It spreads quickly and gains high user engagement, which can harm social unity.
